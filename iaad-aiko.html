01 — AIKO Space

AIKO Space · Turin · October 2024 – April 2025

Designing worlds
for satellites
to learn from.

AIKO is a Turin-based deep-tech startup that builds AI software for autonomous satellites — software that lets spacecraft take decisions in real time, detect anomalies, and operate without waiting for commands from Earth.

My role as Designer & Developer was an unusual one for a company full of aerospace engineers: I built virtual reality environments used to train AIKO's AI. The idea is that before a model goes to orbit, it needs to learn somewhere — and that somewhere was a world I designed.

VR Development Visual Design Social Communication AI Training Environments
Details of the VR project are confidential. What I can say: it was the most technically ambitious thing I've built — and also the most fun.

A full superyacht concept developed from the exterior silhouette down to interior cabin details. The design language contrasts a sharp, minimal hull with warm, residential interiors — making the yacht feel like a home that happens to float.

The hull at dusk — one figure on the deck, the rest is silence. Below: the main lounge, where the circular ceiling trace anchors the whole room.
Dining area, lounge, and detail views. The red accent ring on the table becomes a recurring motif throughout the yacht's interior language.
Details: the spherical lamp in warm light, and the dining area in cooler tones. Light as the main design material. IAAD — Blender + Rendering

Project 02 — Thesis Project

A home
that moves.

Type
Micro-living · Mobility
Graduated
July 2024
Tools
Blender · Rendering · Sketches

My thesis project is a rethinking of the camper van as a living unit — fully autonomous, modular, and designed for long-term inhabitation. The exterior breaks with every convention of the category. The interior is thought from the inside out: how do you live well in 7.35 metres?

Introduction video — thesis presentation
Top view — the full floor plan in one shot. Bedroom module to the left, living and kitchen to the right, open-air deck extension.
Section cutaway with human scale reference — 3100mm height, 7350mm length. The interior is compressed but never compromised.
Interior details: the dining module with its palette of primary colours, the sleeping and kitchen zone, and a night-time render of the experience inside. Thesis — IAAD Turin, 2024

Project 03 — Automotive Design

City car
concept.

Type
Automotive · Urban Mobility
Tools
Blender · Rendering · Sketches
Course
IAAD Turin

A compact city car concept that pushes the proportions of the urban vehicle toward something more expressive and less apologetic. The floral wheel design, the panoramic greenhouse, the asymmetric body — all deliberate provocations against the idea that a small car must be invisible.

Front and rear — two versions of the same language. The contrast between the rounded front and the cut-out rear was one of the key design decisions.
Side profile and rear three-quarter. The floral wheel centres were an early sketch detail that survived all the way to the final model — a small obsession that became the car's signature. IAAD — Blender + Rendering
