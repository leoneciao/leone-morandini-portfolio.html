Photography — Personal Project — Paris, March 2026

Erasmus
in Paris.

I went to visit Arianna, my friend doing her Erasmus in Paris. Three days, her city, her life — and a red notebook she hadn't bought yet.

March 2026
20 photographs
Paris, France
01

Arianna has been living in Paris
since September. I came to see
what that looks like.

She has a flat somewhere between the 9th and the 18th. There are shoes by the door — Salomon trail runners, which feel slightly wrong for Paris and completely right for her. She studies on the sofa with her laptop on her knees. She knows which boulangerie opens first.

I was there for three days in March 2026. This is what I photographed.

Her
apartment.

The story starts inside. Shoes by the door, laptop on the sofa, the particular light of a Parisian flat in early March. This is where she lives now.

Salomon trail runners in Paris. She puts them on every morning like a ritual. On the sofa, studying — the ordinary version of Erasmus they don't put on the brochure. Her apartment, 9th arr.
"

Erasmus is mostly
a sofa, a laptop, and
a city outside the window.

— Her apartment, March 2026

Then we
go out.

Her building has a spiral staircase. Of course it does — it's Paris. We leave every morning like we're descending into something.

The staircase of her building. Six floors. Every time, a different angle. 9th arrondissement, March 2026
The streets at 7am before Paris wakes up. Haussmann buildings in golden light. A lamppost nobody asked to be perfect. Paris streets, early morning

Montmartre,
her territory.

She's been here two months. She knows which streets to avoid and which ones to take on purpose. Montmartre is one of her places — she walks it like someone who has already stopped being a tourist.

I followed her up the stairs with my camera. She didn't wait for me.

Montmartre — Rue Lepic area

Sacré-Cœur before the crowds. She had already been. She came again anyway. Montmartre, 7:30am

The
red notebook.

She bought it at the bouquinistes along the Seine. Used books, 2€ each, and a notebook she didn't need but couldn't leave behind. She carried it the rest of the day.

The bouquinistes — she spent twenty minutes on one table. That same afternoon, a fromagerie caught the morning light like it knew it was being photographed. Along the Seine & Paris streets
The city in between: a man and his dog at the Tuileries, basketball under the Tour Eiffel, a red jacket against a white wall, a carousel nobody was riding. Paris — various locations
"

She said she bought it
because the cover was red.
That was enough of a reason.

— Bouquinistes, Seine riverbank, March 2026

Paris
after dark.

We stayed out late. She still had the notebook. The streets were louder. The light was different. She moved like she owned the place — because at this point, she kind of did.

Moulin Rouge at dusk — she had walked past it a hundred times and still stopped to look. Pigalle, evening
She moves fast at night — hence the blur. The red notebook under the pharmacy cross. And the window of her apartment, from the inside, before sleep. Paris by night, March 2026

Her city.
My visit.

On the last day we went to the Trocadéro. Then I went alone to the Louvre and did the thing you do at the Louvre. She stayed home. She's already been.

She was laughing at something. The Eiffel Tower was just there, behind her, like it always is. I went to the Louvre alone and pointed at a pyramid — the natural response. Trocadéro & Louvre, March 2026
"

She's been there six months.
I was there three days.
She showed me everything.
I still feel like I missed half.

— Leone Morandini, March 2026
