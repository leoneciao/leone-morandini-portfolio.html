Photography — Personal Project — Paris, March 2026

Erasmus
in Paris.

I went to visit Arianna, my friend doing her Erasmus in Paris. Three days, her city, her life and a red notebook she hadn't bought yet.

March 2026
20 photographs
Paris, France
01

Arianna has been living in Paris
since January. I came to see
what that looks like.

She has a flat somewhere between the 9th and the 18th. There are shoes by the door: Salomon trail runners, which feel slightly wrong for Paris and completely right for her. sometimes she studies on the sofa with her laptop on her knees. She knows which boulangerie opens first.

I was there for three days in March 2026. This is what I photographed.

Her
apartment.

The story starts inside. Shoes by the door, laptop on the sofa, morning light through the window. This is where she lives now.

Studying on the sofa. Her two flatmates folding a blanket by the window; ordinary moments from an Erasmus flat in the 17th. Her apartment, March 2026
"

Erasmus is mostly
a sofa, a laptop, and
a city outside the window.

— Her apartment, March 2026

Then we
go out.

Her building has a spiral staircase. Of course it does, it's Paris. We leave every morning through it. Then the city begins.

Her staircase: five floors, every time a different angle. Then outside: Haussmann buildings catching the first light. Her building & Paris streets
An empty intersection at 10am. A fromagerie that looks like it was designed by someone who loved type. A lamppost nobody asked to be perfect. Paris streets, early morning

Montmartre,
her territory.

She's been here three months. She knows which streets to avoid and which ones to take on purpose. Montmartre is one of her places: she walks it like someone who has already stopped being a tourist.

I followed her up the stairs with my camera. She didn't wait for me.

Montmartre — Rue Lepic area

Sacré-Cœur before the crowds. She had already been. She came again anyway. Montmartre, 4:30pm

The
red notebook.

She bought it at the bouquinistes along the Champ de Mars. Used books, 2€ each, and a notebook she didn't need but couldn't leave behind. She carried it the rest of the day.

The bouquinistes: she spent twenty minutes on one table. A man with his dog at the Tuileries, indifferent to everything, which is the most Parisian thing possible. Seine riverbank & Tuileries
A red jacket against a white wall. Basketball in the shadow of the Eiffel Tower. A carousel nobody was riding. Paris - various locations
"

She said she bought it
because the cover was red.
That was enough of a reason.

— Bouquinistes, Seine riverbank, March 2026

Paris
after dark.

We stayed out late. She still had the notebook. The streets were louder. The light was different. She didn't move like she owned the place anymore, because at this point, she is still a girl from the countryside.

Moulin Rouge at dusk: she had walked past it a hundred times and still stopped to look. Pigalle, evening
She moves fast at night, hence the blur. Then, still out, the red notebook under a pharmacy cross. She'd been carrying it all day. Paris by night, March 2026

Her city.
My visit.

On the last day we went to the Trocadéro. Then my friend went to the Louvre. She stayed home. It was time for us to leave.

She was laughing at something. The Eiffel Tower was just there, behind her, like it always is. A friend at the Louvre, doing the thing you do at the Louvre. Trocadéro & Louvre, March 2026
"

She's been there three months.
I was there three days.
She showed me something.
I still feel like I missed almost everything.

— Leone Morandini, March 2026
