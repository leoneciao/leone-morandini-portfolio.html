A small church in Piedmont, an October morning, four generations in the same square. My job was to stay still, watch, and press at the right moment.

The

Some photographs can't be planned. You just wait for them. The church was quiet. And then it happened.

At the altar. The priest said something. He laughed first. Then she did. Then everything else became secondary. Religious ceremony
She turns with those eyes. He wipes his glasses. Outside the church, October light finds them still looking at each other the same way.

Just

When you walk out of a church and hug the person you've been waiting to hug all morning, your face stops pretending. Everything you held in comes out.

Outside the church — Piedmont

Who
was there.

A wedding is never just two people. It's everyone those two people have loved over time, gathered in the same place for a few hours.

The boy reaching toward the old man in the chair — one of the best moments of the day, and nobody else was watching. The grandparents who made a great effort to be there. And they were.
The whole family outside the church door. In the middle of all that happy chaos, she leaned down toward the smallest child. She noticed him. Church square
